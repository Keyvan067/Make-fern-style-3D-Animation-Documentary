\# AI Documentary Channel — AI Generation Guidelines



\---



\# Purpose



This document defines the standards for using Artificial Intelligence tools during documentary production.



The goal is to achieve:



\* Consistent visuals

\* Realistic storytelling

\* Professional quality

\* Efficient production



AI is treated as a production assistant, not a replacement for creative direction.



\---



\# Core AI Philosophy



AI generation must always serve:



\* Story

\* Emotion

\* Historical accuracy

\* Visual consistency



A beautiful image without storytelling value has no purpose.



\---



\# GENERAL GENERATION RULES



\## Rule 01 — Consistency First



Every generated element must remain consistent across the episode.



Maintain:



\* Character identity

\* Environment design

\* Time period

\* Color style

\* Camera language



\---



\## Rule 02 — Reference Before Generation



Before generating new content:



Check:



\* Character files

\* Asset files

\* Blueprint instructions



Never generate from memory.



\---



\## Rule 03 — Realism Over Fantasy



The final result should feel like:



A professionally produced documentary.



Avoid:



\* Unrealistic cinematic effects

\* Fantasy elements

\* Overly dramatic visuals

\* Artificial AI appearance



\---



\# IMAGE GENERATION GUIDELINES



\## Image Purpose



Images are used for:



\* Character references

\* Environment creation

\* Historical reconstruction

\* Evidence visualization



\---



\# Image Prompt Structure



Every image prompt should contain:



```text

Subject



\+



Historical Context



\+



Environment



\+



Lighting



\+



Camera Style



\+



Realism Level

```



\---



\## Example Structure



```text

A 1970s airline passenger inside a Boeing 727 cabin,

authentic historical environment,

natural cabin lighting,

35mm documentary photography,

cinematic realism.

```



\---



\# Image Quality Rules



Prioritize:



\* Natural anatomy

\* Realistic faces

\* Accurate clothing

\* Correct objects

\* Real materials



Avoid:



\* Plastic skin

\* Perfect symmetry

\* Unreal lighting

\* AI artifacts



\---



\# VIDEO GENERATION GUIDELINES



\## Video Purpose



Video generation should focus on:



\* Movement

\* Atmosphere

\* Story progression



Not only beautiful shots.



\---



\# Video Prompt Structure



Every video prompt should define:



```text

Subject



\+



Action



\+



Camera Movement



\+



Environment



\+



Lighting



\+



Mood

```



\---



\# Camera Rules



Preferred:



\* Slow tracking shots

\* Documentary handheld movement

\* Cinematic push-ins

\* Natural camera motion



Avoid:



\* Excessive camera movement

\* Impossible shots

\* Hollywood-style exaggeration



\---



\# Character Motion Rules



Characters should have:



\* Natural walking

\* Realistic gestures

\* Human timing

\* Subtle expressions



Avoid:



\* Fast unnatural movement

\* Overacting

\* Repetitive AI motion



\---



\# VOICE GENERATION GUIDELINES



\## Voice Goal



Narration should feel like a professional documentary.



\---



\# Narrator Characteristics



Voice should be:



\* Calm

\* Intelligent

\* Trustworthy

\* Emotional when necessary



\---



\# Voice Direction System



Every script section should define:



\## Emotion



Examples:



\* Calm

\* Suspenseful

\* Serious

\* Emotional

\* Curious



\---



\## Speed



Examples:



\* Slow

\* Normal

\* Fast



\---



\## Emphasis



Important words should be marked.



Example:



"The aircraft disappeared into the \*\*dark storm\*\*."



\---



\## Pause Control



Use pauses for:



\* Mystery

\* Important reveals

\* Emotional moments



\---



\# MUSIC GENERATION GUIDELINES



\## Music Purpose



Music supports emotion.



It should never overpower narration.



\---



\# Music Styles



Recommended:



\* Ambient documentary

\* Cinematic minimal

\* Suspense atmosphere

\* Emotional orchestral



\---



Avoid:



\* Overdramatic action music

\* Trailer-style music everywhere



\---



\# SOUND DESIGN GUIDELINES



Sound creates immersion.



Every scene should consider:



\* Environment sounds

\* Object sounds

\* Movement sounds

\* Atmospheric layers



\---



\# Example



Airport Scene:



Include:



\* Footsteps

\* Announcements

\* Luggage movement

\* Crowd ambience



\---



\# HISTORICAL ACCURACY RULES



Before generation verify:



\* Clothing

\* Architecture

\* Vehicles

\* Technology

\* Documents

\* Language



No modern elements should appear in historical scenes.



\---



\# AI QUALITY CONTROL



Before accepting any generation check:



\## Visual



\* Does it match the Asset?

\* Is the era correct?

\* Is the lighting consistent?



\---



\## Motion



\* Is movement natural?

\* Are there AI artifacts?



\---



\## Story



\* Does this shot help the narrative?



\---



\# Prompt Management Rules



Never store random prompts.



Every production prompt must belong to:



\* Character file

\* Asset file

\* Blueprint shot



\---



\# File Responsibility



\## Characters



Contains:



Who appears.



\---



\## Assets



Contains:



What exists.



\---



\## Blueprint



Contains:



How it is shown.



\---



\# Final Principle



AI should increase creative capability.



It should not replace:



\* Research

\* Storytelling

\* Direction

\* Human judgment



The final product must feel like a documentary created by a professional studio.



\---



\# End of AI Generation Guidelines
