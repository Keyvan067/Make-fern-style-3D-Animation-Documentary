\# AI Documentary Channel — Channel Bible

\# Channel Identity



\## Channel Name



\[Working Title]



\---



\## Channel Type



Cinematic AI Documentary Channel



\---



\## Core Promise



The channel transforms real events, mysteries and historical cases into immersive cinematic documentaries.



Every episode combines:



\* Deep research

\* Storytelling

\* AI-generated visuals

\* Cinematic sound design

\* Documentary filmmaking techniques



\---



\# Brand Positioning



\## What We Create



High-quality cinematic documentaries about:



\* Unsolved mysteries

\* Historical events

\* True crime cases

\* Disasters

\* Aviation incidents

\* Hidden stories



\---



\## What We Avoid



The channel does not create:



\* Low-effort AI videos

\* Fake news

\* Sensational misinformation

\* Pure speculation presented as fact

\* Clickbait without substance



\---



\# Audience



\## Primary Audience



People interested in:



\* Mystery

\* History

\* Investigation

\* Science

\* Human stories

\* Real-world events



\---



\## Viewer Psychology



The audience enjoys:



\* Discovering unknown stories

\* Solving mysteries

\* Understanding human decisions

\* Exploring historical events



\---



\# Storytelling Style



\## Narrative Voice



The narrator should feel:



\* Intelligent

\* Calm

\* Curious

\* Trustworthy

\* Cinematic



\---



\## Writing Style



The script should use:



\* Simple but powerful language

\* Strong visual descriptions

\* Emotional pacing

\* Suspense building



Avoid:



\* Overdramatic language

\* False claims

\* Excessive exaggeration



\---



\# Episode Structure



Every episode follows this structure:



\---



\## 1. Cold Open



Purpose:



Capture attention immediately.



Elements:



\* Mystery

\* Powerful question

\* Unexpected event



Duration:



First 30–60 seconds



\---



\## 2. Background



Introduce:



\* Time

\* Place

\* Main people

\* Historical context



\---



\## 3. The Event



Explain:



\* What happened

\* How it happened

\* Who was involved



\---



\## 4. Investigation



Explore:



\* Evidence

\* Research

\* Discoveries

\* Official investigations



\---



\## 5. Theories



Present:



\* Different explanations

\* Supporting evidence

\* Remaining questions



\---



\## 6. Conclusion



End with:



\* Reflection

\* Open questions

\* Emotional impact

\---



\# Sound Identity



Audio is a major storytelling element.



Every episode should include:



\* Professional narration

\* Ambient environments

\* Realistic sound effects

\* Cinematic background music



\---



\# Thumbnail Philosophy



Thumbnails must be:



\* Simple

\* Emotional

\* Recognizable

\* High contrast



Avoid:



\* Overloaded text

\* Fake expressions

\* Misleading visuals



\---



\# Title Philosophy



Titles should create curiosity without deception.



Examples:



Good:



"The Man Who Hijacked a Plane and Vanished Forever"



"The Mystery That Remained Unsolved for 50 Years"



Avoid:



"He Was NEVER Found!!! SHOCKING SECRET!!!"



\---



\# Channel Personality



The channel behaves like:



A professional documentary studio.



Not:



A content farm.



\---



\# Quality Rules



Before publishing any episode:



Check:



\* Is the story accurate?

\* Are sources reliable?

\* Are visuals consistent?

\* Is narration engaging?

\* Does the ending leave an impression?
