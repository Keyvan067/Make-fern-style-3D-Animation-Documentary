\# EP001 — The Disappearance of D. B. Cooper



\---



\## Episode Information



\*\*Estimated Duration:\*\* 15–18 Minutes



\*\*Narration Style:\*\* Cinematic Documentary



\*\*Language:\*\* English (US)



\*\*Objective:\*\* Tell the story through suspense while remaining historically accurate.



\---



\# COLD OPEN



A man walks onto a commercial airplane carrying nothing more than a black briefcase.



No one notices him.



No one remembers seeing him.



Within the next few hours...



He will hijack an airplane...



Collect two hundred thousand dollars...



Jump into a violent storm...



And disappear forever.



More than fifty years later...



No one knows who he really was.



This is the story of...



D. B. Cooper.



\---



\# CHAPTER 1 — AN ORDINARY PASSENGER



November 24th, 1971.



The day before Thanksgiving.



Portland International Airport.



Business travelers hurry toward their flights.



Families prepare for the holiday.



Everything appears completely ordinary.



Among the passengers is a quiet man wearing a dark business suit, a white shirt, a black tie and sunglasses.



He buys a one-way ticket under the name \*\*Dan Cooper\*\*.



He carries only a briefcase.



Nothing about him attracts attention.



At exactly 2:50 PM, Northwest Orient Flight 305 departs for Seattle.



No one onboard realizes they are about to become part of history.



\---



\# CHAPTER 2 — THE NOTE



Shortly after takeoff, Cooper orders a bourbon and soda.



Moments later, he hands a folded note to flight attendant Florence Schaffner.



She assumes it is another businessman flirting with her.



Without reading it, she slips it into her purse.



Cooper quietly leans toward her.



\*"Miss... you'd better read the note."\*



The message is short.



\*"I have a bomb."\*



Within seconds...



An ordinary flight becomes a hijacking.



\---



\# CHAPTER 3 — A MAN IN COMPLETE CONTROL



Unlike most hijackers...



Cooper never raises his voice.



He never threatens the passengers.



He never panics.



Instead, he calmly explains exactly what he wants.



Two hundred thousand dollars.



Four parachutes.



A fuel truck waiting in Seattle.



Nothing more.



Nothing less.



When Florence sits beside him, Cooper opens his briefcase just enough for her to see several red cylinders, wires and a battery.



Whether it is a real bomb...



No one will ever know.



\---



\# CHAPTER 4 — THE NEGOTIATION



As Flight 305 circles above Seattle, authorities race against time.



The FBI.



Local police.



Airline officials.



Everyone works to meet Cooper's demands.



When the aircraft lands, the money is delivered.



The parachutes arrive.



One by one, every passenger leaves the airplane safely.



Only the flight crew remains.



And Cooper is still in control.



\---



\# CHAPTER 5 — INTO THE STORM



The aircraft takes off once again.



Its destination, according to Cooper...



Mexico City.



But first, a refueling stop in Reno.



Then Cooper makes several unusual requests.



Fly low.



Fly slow.



Keep the landing gear partially extended.



Lower the flaps.



These are not random instructions.



They suggest someone familiar with the Boeing 727.



Outside...



Night has fallen.



Heavy rain covers the Pacific Northwest.



Strong winds shake the aircraft.



The conditions are terrible.



For almost anyone...



Jumping would mean certain death.



\---



\# CHAPTER 6 — THE JUMP



At approximately 8:13 PM...



The cockpit instruments register a sudden movement.



The aircraft briefly shifts upward.



The rear staircase has been lowered.



None of the crew sees Cooper leave.



No distress call is heard.



No parachute is ever spotted.



Only darkness.



Only rain.



Only silence.



When the aircraft lands in Reno...



Cooper is gone.



\---



\# CHAPTER 7 — THE HUNT



The FBI launches one of the largest investigations in its history.



The operation receives the code name...



NORJAK.



Thousands of suspects are investigated.



Former soldiers.



Pilots.



Skydivers.



Criminals.



The forests are searched.



The rivers are searched.



The mountains are searched.



Nothing.



No body.



No parachute.



No trace.



\---



\# CHAPTER 8 — A SMALL DISCOVERY



Nine years later...



A young boy named Brian Ingram is playing on the banks of the Columbia River.



While digging in the sand, he uncovers several bundles of decaying twenty-dollar bills.



The serial numbers match the ransom money.



Exactly $5,800.



It is the only confirmed piece of the ransom ever recovered.



The rest...



Has never been found.



\---



\# CHAPTER 9 — THEORIES



Did Cooper die during the jump?



Did he survive and disappear under a new identity?



Was he a military parachutist?



An airline employee?



An intelligence operative?



Or simply an ordinary man who planned the perfect crime?



Decades of investigation have produced countless theories...



But no definitive answer.



\---



\# FINAL REFLECTION



The most remarkable part of this story...



Is not that a man hijacked an airplane.



It is that he may have escaped without leaving behind a single answer.



No confirmed identity.



No arrest.



No confession.



No grave.



Only questions.



History remembers him as...



D. B. Cooper.



Yet the name on the ticket he purchased that afternoon...



Was not D. B. Cooper.



It was...



Dan Cooper.



And perhaps that is the greatest mystery of all.
