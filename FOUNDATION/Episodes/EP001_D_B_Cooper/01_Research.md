\# Executive Summary



On November 24, 1971, a man using the alias \*\*Dan Cooper\*\* boarded Northwest Orient Flight 305 in Portland, Oregon.



Shortly after takeoff, he informed a flight attendant that he was carrying a bomb and demanded \*\*$200,000 in cash\*\* along with \*\*four parachutes\*\*.



After the aircraft landed in Seattle, all passengers were released, the ransom was delivered, and the plane departed again with only the flight crew and Cooper onboard.



Somewhere over the Pacific Northwest, Cooper lowered the Boeing 727's rear airstair and parachuted into the night carrying the ransom.



Despite one of the largest investigations in FBI history, no verified trace of him was ever found.



More than fifty years later, the case remains officially unsolved.

\# Complete Timeline



\## Boarding



\* Purchased a one-way ticket using cash.

\* Used the name \*\*Dan Cooper\*\*.

\* Carried only a black briefcase.



\---



\## Shortly After Takeoff



\* Ordered bourbon and soda.

\* Handed a folded note to Florence Schaffner.



\---



\## Bomb Threat



The note claimed he had a bomb.



When requested, Cooper partially opened his briefcase.



Witnesses described seeing:



\* Red cylindrical objects

\* Wires

\* Batteries



The bomb was never recovered.



Its authenticity remains unknown.



\---



\## Demands



Cooper requested:



\* $200,000 in cash

\* Four parachutes

\* Refueling truck



No additional political or ideological demands were made.



\---



\## Seattle Landing



\* Passengers released safely.

\* Ransom delivered.

\* Parachutes delivered.

\* Aircraft refueled.



Only Cooper and the flight crew remained onboard.



\---



\## Second Flight



Cooper instructed the pilots to maintain:



\* Low altitude

\* Slow airspeed

\* Landing gear partially extended

\* Wing flaps deployed

\* Rear stairs available



These requests suggested detailed knowledge of the Boeing 727.



\---



\## The Jump



Approximate Time:



\*\*8:13 PM\*\*



Cockpit instruments detected movement.



Rear stairs were lowered.



A pressure change was recorded.



No one witnessed Cooper leaving the aircraft.



\---



\## Landing at Reno



The aircraft landed safely.



Cooper was gone.



Only unused equipment remained onboard.

\# Money



Every ransom bill had its serial number recorded by the FBI.



In 1980, Brian Ingram discovered \*\*$5,800\*\* buried along the Columbia River.



No additional ransom money has ever been confirmed.
